Stereo Computer Vision Research Project

This is a research project I worked on for an independent research science class. It involved testing different subpixel measurement algorithms in conjunction with stereo computer vision (currently using opencv and python), to see how much I could decrease the baseline between the two cameras while still maintaining a reasonable resolution in my distance measurements. The eventual end goal is to create a system that uses small shifts in position of lightweight camera platforms to simulate stereo computer vision.
Created using frozen-flask by Samuel Schuur, 2019.
